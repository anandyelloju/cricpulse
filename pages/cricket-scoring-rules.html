Cricket scoring rules

Cricket scoring records every run, legal delivery, wicket, extra, and innings total. A score such as 86/4 means the batting team has scored 86 runs and lost 4 wickets.

Runs and wickets

Runs come from batter shots, running between wickets, boundaries, and extras. Wickets are recorded when a batter is dismissed by bowled, caught, run out, stumped, hit wicket, or another valid dismissal.

Overs

An over has six legal balls. Wides and no balls usually add runs but do not count as legal deliveries, so the bowler must bowl another ball.

Scorecards

A complete cricket scorecard includes team total, wickets, overs, batter runs, balls faced, boundaries, bowler overs, runs conceded, wickets, economy rate, and over history.
