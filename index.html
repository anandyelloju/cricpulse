What is CricPulse?

CricPulse is a free online cricket score calculator built for street cricket, school matches, club fixtures, box cricket, and practice games. It helps scorers record every ball, manage innings, calculate run rate, track extras, and prepare a clean cricket scorecard from a phone.

Why use CricPulse?

Paper scorebooks work, but live cricket scoring is faster when the scorer has run buttons, extras, wickets, bowler changes, and over history in one place. CricPulse keeps the scoring flow simple so local matches can focus on the game.

Mobile scoring benefits

The interface is designed for one-handed scoring on mobile. Large buttons, quick match setup, visible striker and bowler details, and persistent controls reduce mistakes during fast overs.

How scoring works

A legal delivery adds one ball to the over. Batter runs increase the team total and the batter score. Extras such as wides and no balls add runs without always counting as legal balls. Wickets reduce the batting side's available players and may require the next batter to come in.

Cricket scoring guide

For a simple local match, set the teams, choose overs, complete the toss, pick the striker, non-striker, and opening bowler, then score ball by ball. CricPulse updates the live scorecard, current over, run rate, and player statistics automatically.

Cricket scoring FAQ

How do I calculate cricket score online?

Open CricPulse, enter teams and overs, run the toss, then tap the matching run, extra, or wicket button after every ball. The app updates total runs, wickets, overs, and run rate.

How does cricket run rate work?

Run rate is total runs divided by overs faced. If a team scores 60 runs in 10 overs, the run rate is 6.00 runs per over.

How are wides and no balls calculated?

A wide or no ball normally adds one extra run to the batting team. Additional runs can also be added, and these deliveries usually do not count as legal balls in the over.
